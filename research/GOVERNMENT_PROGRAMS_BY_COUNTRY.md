## North America

### United States

#### SBIR/STTR Programs

**Agency:** Multiple Federal Agencies
**Budget:** $200+ million annually (NSF alone)
**Funding Range:**
- Phase I: Up to $305,000 (6-18 months)
- Phase II: Up to $2,095,748 (24 months)

**Participating Agencies:**
- Department of Defense (DOD): ~2,000 awards/year
- National Institutes of Health (NIH): ~800 awards/year
- National Science Foundation (NSF): ~400 awards/year
- Department of Energy (DOE): ~300 awards/year
- NASA: ~200 awards/year
- USDA: ~100 awards/year
- Department of Homeland Security (DHS): ~150 awards/year
- NOAA: ~50 awards/year

**Key Benefits:**
- Non-dilutive funding (no equity required)
- No repayment required
- Retain full IP rights

**Eligibility:**
- US-based for-profit small business
- ≤500 employees
- >50% owned by US citizens or permanent residents

**Website:** https://www.sbir.gov/
**API Available:** Yes

**Status (2026):** NSF SBIR/STTR temporarily paused due to congressional authorization lapse. Expected to resume in 2026.

---

#### America's Seed Fund (NSF SBIR/STTR)

**Agency:** National Science Foundation
**Funding:** Up to $305,000 (Phase I), up to $2M+ (Phase II)
**Focus Areas:** Deep tech, AI, robotics, biotech, cleantech

**Application Timeline:** Rolling basis, 5-7 month review process
**Website:** https://seedfund.nsf.gov/
**Contact:** info@nsf.gov

---

### Canada

#### NRC IRAP (Industrial Research Assistance Program)

**Agency:** National Research Council Canada
**Funding:** Up to $10 million per project
**Coverage:** 60-80% of internal technical labor and subcontractor expenses

**Eligibility:**
- Incorporated profit-oriented SME in Canada
- ≤500 full-time equivalent employees
- Focus on innovative technology development or commercialization

**Specialized Programs:**
- **Clean Technology Support:** Accelerates development, demonstration, and commercialization of clean tech
- **Intellectual Property Support:** IP advisory services and strategy development

**Transition Note:** NRC IRAP will transition to Canada Innovation Corporation by 2026-27

**Website:** https://nrc.canada.ca/en/support-technology-innovation
**Contact:**
- Email: info@nrc-cnrc.gc.ca
- Phone: 1-877-994-4727

## Europe

### European Union (Multi-Country)

#### EIC Accelerator

**Agency:** European Innovation Council
**2026 Budget:** €1.4 billion total
- EIC Accelerator Open: €384 million
- EIC Accelerator Challenges: €220 million
- EIC Pathfinder: €362 million

**Funding Range:**
- Lump sum contributions: Up to €2.5 million (grant-only for startups)
- Total funding: €1-10 million per company
- Pathfinder grants: Up to €4 million for research teams

**Focus Areas:**
- AI and digital technologies (€221 million for AI)
- Cleantech and climate action
- Biotechnology
- Space technology
- Quantum computing and photonics

**Eligibility:**
- Startups and SMEs in EU Member States or Horizon Europe associated countries
- Particularly welcomes applications from female CEOs
- Technology readiness levels (TRL) 6-8

**Website:** https://eic.ec.europa.eu/
**Application Deadline:** Rolling, with specific challenge deadlines

---

#### Horizon Europe Digital & AI Funding

**Budget:** €307.3 million (2026)
- €221 million for trustworthy AI services
- Funding for robotics, quantum tech, photonics, virtual worlds

**Application Window:** Open until April 15, 2026

**Website:** https://eismea.ec.europa.eu/

---

#### EIT InnoEnergy

**Agency:** European Institute of Innovation and Technology
**Focus:** Sustainable energy startups
**Support:** Funding, business coaching, access to 500+ industry partners

**Sectors:**
- Renewable energy
- Energy storage
- Smart grids
- Energy efficiency

---

#### ACCEND (Cross-Border Accelerator)

**Focus:** Deep-tech startups scaling across Europe
**Support:** 100+ startups
**Benefits:**
- Cross-border accelerator programs
- Access to funding

- Global growth coaching
- Public sector matchmaking

### United Kingdom

#### Innovate UK Growth Catalyst

**Agency:** Innovate UK (part of UKRI)
**Funding:** Up to £900,000 per company
**Structure:** Grant funding + aligned private investment + structured growth support

**Focus Sectors:**
- Advanced manufacturing
- Clean energy
- Creative industries
- Defence and security
- Digital technologies
- Life sciences
- Foundation industries

**Target:** High-potential UK startups and scaleups in late-stage innovation

**Website:** https://www.ukri.org/councils/innovate-uk/

---

#### CyberASAP Programme

**Agency:** Department for Science, Innovation and Technology (DSIT)
**Funding:** £10 million (4-year commitment)
**Results to Date:**
- Supported academics from 80+ UK universities
- Formed 42 companies
- Alumni raised £47.4 million in follow-on investment

**Focus:** Cyber security academic startups

---

#### Smart Grants

**Agency:** Innovate UK
**Funding:** Varies by competition (example: £350,000 for manufacturing)
**Focus:** Innovative projects across sectors including quantum, agriculture, sustainable materials

**Examples:**
- Quantum innovations: Up to £3 million available
- Farming/agriculture: Up to £5 million for on-farm trials
- Sustainable building materials

**Website:** https://apply-for-innovation-funding.service.gov.uk/

---

#### Digital Inclusion Innovation Fund

**Launch:** 2025
**Total Budget:** £9.5 million
**Grant Range:** £25,000 - £500,000

### Germany

#### EXIST Programme

**Funding:**
- Up to €3,000/month for living expenses
- €30,000 for material costs
- €5,000 for coaching

**Target:** Students, graduates, and researchers with innovative business ideas
**Support:** Development of prototypes, MVPs, and business plans
**Applications:** Accepted on a rolling basis

#### KfW Venture Capital Programs

**Agency:** KfW (state-owned development bank)
**Partnership:** Franco-German cooperation with Bpifrance
**Investment:** Up to €1 billion in public-private investments for Franco-German VC funds

## Usage Notes

**For Scraping:**
- Most programs publish award recipients publicly
- Some have APIs (particularly US programs)
- Others publish annual reports or press releases
- Check each program's website for "Awards", "Recipients", or "Portfolio" pages

**For Enrichment:**
- Government-funded companies often appear in Crunchbase/PitchBook
- Cross-reference company names with funding databases
- Government funding is a strong signal for follow-on private investment

**For Analysis:**
- Track companies that receive both government and private funding
- Identify geographic trends (which countries/regions most active)
- Correlate government program participation with success metrics
